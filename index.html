But what happens when we step back and look at the bigger picture?

The Bigger Picture: Trends Over Time

The extreme examples make critics and audiences seem far apart, but the broader trends tell a different story. Across decades, both groups show remarkably similar long-term patterns in how they rate movies.

Average Ratings Over Time

Over the past century, critics and audiences have shown very similar patterns in how they rate movies.
After the 1980s, their curves run nearly in parallel, likely because people had greater access to movies and more chances to discuss them.
This suggests that both groups tend to react in similar ways to major changes in cinema.

The Bigger Picture: Patterns Across Genres

Critics and audiences tend to rate movies in similar ways, not just across decades, but across genres as well.

Genre-Level Differences

Across genres, critics and audiences show strong overall agreement, with average score gaps under 6%. Critics tend to prefer Animation and Comedy, while audiences favor Fantasy and Musicals.

Within-Genre Agreement

Within each genre, most films cluster close to the diagonal equality line, revealing that critics and audiences often converge on what they consider strong or weak titles.

So... Who Should You Trust?

After analyzing thousands of films across genres and decades, one pattern stands out: critics and audiences agree far more often than the disagreements suggest. You don't have to choose ‚Äî both groups usually point to the same qualities in a good movie.
